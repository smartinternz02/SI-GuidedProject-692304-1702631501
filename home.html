The Credit Card Customer Segmentation project leverages Principal Component Analysis (PCA)and K-means clustering to uncover valuable insights from a dataset containing customercredit card usage information. PCA is employed to reduce the dimensionality of the data,identifying the most significant features and transforming them into a new set of uncorrelated variables.This dimensionality reduction simplifies the dataset while retaining essential information. Subsequently,K-means clustering is applied to categorize customers into distinct segments based on their credit card usage patterns, spending behaviors, and preferences. By identifying these customer segments, financial institutions can tailor their services, marketing strategies, and product offerings to better meet the diverse needs of their clientele, ultimately enhancing customer satisfaction and loyalty while optimizing business performance.
